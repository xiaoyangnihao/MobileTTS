Audio samples from "MobileTTS: A deployable and productive text-to-speech network for mobile devices"

Text-to-speech is widely applied to different scenarios, among which the demand on mobile devices is increasingly prominent. However, current popular frameworks are not well-deployed to mobile devices, due to low computing power, small memory, etc. MobileTTS is proposed in this paper, a fully convolutional, deployable and productive network for mobile devices. To ensure the model lightweight and fast inference speed, two lightweight architectures: depthwise separable convolution (SepConv) and stacked Deep Feedforward Sequential Memory Network block (SDB) are introduced. While AlignmentEncoder is adopted to obtain phone-level duration during training to remove the dependency on an external speech-text alignment tool. Quantization aware training and compression is first introduced to speech synthesis. Experiments show the proposed MobileTTS can achieve satisfied performance with 6.41x inference speedup on CPU and 18.9x compression ratio with 1.8M model parameters and 2.2M model size. In most mobile device scenarios, MobileTTS can run smoothly and generate high-quality audios.
